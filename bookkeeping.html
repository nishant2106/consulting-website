What is Bookkeeping?

The process of recording, compiling, and organizing a business or company's daily financial transactions is known as bookkeeping.
Bookkeeping Service Include:

  1. Framing Bookkeeping Policies
  2. Preparing and maintenance Chart of accounts
  3. Posting and Processing of Accounting Entries
  4. Finalization of Books of Accounts
  5. Preparation of Financials Statements

Why Outsource the Bookkeeping Process?

When it comes to accurate accounting management, tracking accounts receivables and payables along with maintenance of financial books, it is an essential but difficult task. It's difficult to manage such items without the help of a support team that specializes in Bookkeeping Services.

With increased competition to establish a startup or maintain an existing business, an organized system for accounting, bookkeeping, and keeping track of the numbers is a must, and qualified professional help from specialists is certain to come in handy.

Bookkeeping Outsourcing Service enables you to benefit from cutting-edge technology and highly skilled personnel without having to invest in them or purchase and maintain high-cost software.

Consider delegation of bookkeeping activity to an outsourcing firm if you have a small team that handles the bookkeeping. This will allow you to focus more time on growing your business. Furthermore, if you're a larger company, it can save you the time required to hire, train, and manage an in-house accounting team.

What is accounts Payable

"Accounts payable" (AP) is a general ledger account that shows an organization's obligation to repay a short-term debt to creditors or suppliers. Another typical meaning of "AP" is the business department or division in charge of making payments to suppliers and other creditors on behalf of the company.

One of the most essential benefits of having strong accounts payable services providers such as Acco Global on your side is that it aids in the improvement of your supplier relationships. All of your invoices will be cleared well ahead of schedule, and you may review these reports on a monthly or quarterly basis to devise business-improvement strategies. In addition, discrepancies in invoices and purchase orders can be prevented entirely with a competent and timely accounts payable system in place. Major processes for AP outsourcing include:

  1. Supplier master Management
  2. Purchase Order Processing
  3. Invoice Processing
  4. Payable tracking & Reporting
  5. Vendor Payments
  6. Reconciliations and Accounting of Payables

3. General Accounting

Record to report (R2R) is a finance and accounting management process that involves collecting, processing and presenting accurate financial data. R2R provides strategic, financial and operational feedback on the performance of the organization to inform management and other stakeholders.

  • Journals – Processing manual journal entries is a service that necessitates both knowledge and experience. Outsourcing accounting services such as manual journal entries could result in error-free manual journal entries as well as help your business managers be more productive. Journals could be of various types for eg., Prepayment and Amortization of Expenses, Reclassification of Expenses, Depreciation, Accruals and Provisions for various Expenses.
  • Month/Quarter/Year end Services -An accounting method used by accountants to close the accounting records for the current accounting period at the conclusion of each reporting period. When a period is marked as closed, no new entries will be accepted during that time. It assures that all financial transactions from the closed period have been accounted for. At each reporting period being a month/quarter/year end Patience, knowledge, and professional experience are required. The period closure process is a time-consuming process that requires you to collect, evaluate, reconcile, and change data from multiple systems. The process's complexity stems in part from the necessity for coordination across several functions in order to perform their tasks on time.
  • Reconciliations – Each month/Quarter/six monthly/year, when the bank statement/statement of inventory/creditor/debtor arrives, reconciliation should be done. It helps in the evaluation of revenue and expenses, as well as the reconciliation of what the other party has recorded with what you have documented. Following are major areas where reconciliation is required:
    1. Creditors Provide Inventory (Creditors to Inventory)
    2. Inventory is sold to Debtors (Inventory to Debtors)
    3. Debtors make payments against sales (Debtors to Cash & Bank)
    4. Cash and Bank is used to pay Creditors against purchases (Cash & Bank to Creditors)
  • Variance Analysis – Most businesses today keep track of their finances with a budget. The monthly closing procedure includes tracking monthly expenses against budget and explaining notable variances. To obtain an accurate picture of the company's financial situation, a comparison of actual to budget should be made. Variance analysis can aid in the correction of errors and the posting of appropriate adjustments to prepaid and accrued charges. Variance Analysis can be done for Year to Date, Quarter to Date, Month to date and Year on Year.
  • Reporting and Review – After completing the above functions, it is important to have a reporting mechanism for a panoramic view for the financial status of an organization. Reporting of the transactions plays a crucial role as it helps not only organization but also other stakeholders such as investors, auditors, government, shareholders, etc to evaluate an organization. Once reporting is completed, it is very important to have a review of the financial status and other aspects so that necessary measures (if any) for future periods.
